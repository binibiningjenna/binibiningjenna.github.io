About planets

The solar system is composed of eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. In addition, there are five recognized dwarf planets: Ceres, Pluto, Haumea, Makemake, and Eris. The planets vary in size, composition, and distance from the Sun, with gas giants like Jupiter and Saturn being much larger than the rocky inner planets. The solar system also contains a variety of other celestial objects, including moons, comets, and the Kuiper Belt.
